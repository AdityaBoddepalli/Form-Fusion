About Us

Starting the journey of personal improvement can be a difficult task whether it is improving one's stretching form, lifting form, throwing form, ballet form, etc. We all have various activities where simply having a guide could greatly improve the efficiency of our training, so our goal with FormFusion was to provide an easy to use application that gives feedback on the users form. FormFusion takes in the users personal video and any professional reference video and compares the user's form against the professional's. It then returns feedback about areas where the user's form diverges from the professional's. This allows for quick feedback for the user to then redo the activity and apply the given feedback, increasing the efficiency of their training. The user will also be able to move to different points in each video and visually compare the differences between forms for a specific area in the activity where they might be unsure of what change to make. We hope that through this software those that need guidance but may not have the time or resources to employ a personal trainer or coach for their activity can still have access to feedback so they don't have to blindly try and improve at their hobby.

What We Used

FormFusion’s primary functionality is achieved through OpenCV and Google’s MediaPipe framework. These tools make for a capable AI-empowered API that works by analyzing a video file uploaded to an S3 bucket, and overlaying 33 landmark points onto the video’s subject. This enables an effective visualization of joint angles with which to analyze and determine the quality of your form! See our GitHub repository here.

Try it out!

Below you will find a quick form to complete! FormFusion works best by providing a video of your movement to compare against an expert-led video of the same movement. This way, you can better observe what to work on. First, select your video file and the expert-completed video file. Then, choose the appropriate joints to include in the analysis in the checklist that follows. Generally, categorize your exercise as upper-body or lower-body, and choose relevant joints. For example, if choosing a pull-up to analyze, you’d want upper-body joins such as the elbows and shoulders!
